### 3. Rigorous Wording Norms for Conclusion

*   **Prohibited Absolute Claims**:
    *   ❌ "proves" → ✅ "demonstrates," "establishes," "supports"
    *   ❌ "undoubtedly" → ✅ "strongly suggests," "indicates"
    *   ❌ "solves" → ✅ "addresses," "mitigates," "improves"
    *   ❌ "always" → ✅ "typically," "often," "under these conditions"

*   **Prohibited Redundancy**:
    *   ❌ Repeating specific p-values or percentages from Results.
    *   ❌ Restating the entire abstract verbatim.
    *   ❌ Re-listing all limitations detailed in Discussion.

*   **Required Precision**:
    *   Ensure the scope of the conclusion matches the scope of the study (e.g., do not generalize from mice to humans without qualification).
    *   Ensure the tone matches the strength of the evidence (e.g., observational vs. experimental).

## V. Pitfall Avoidance Guide and Quality Checklist

### 1. Pitfall Avoidance Guide

*   **Repetition Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Copying the first paragraph of Discussion into Conclusion.
    *   **Fix**: Rewrite the core finding using broader, more synthesizing language. Focus on the "verdict" rather than the "argument."

*   **New Information Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Introducing a new implication or finding in the Conclusion.
    *   **Fix**: Ensure every claim in the Conclusion is grounded in the Discussion and Results.

*   **Disconnect Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Conclusion does not answer the Introduction's research question.
    *   **Fix**: Explicitly reference the initial problem statement in the Conclusion opening.

*   **Overstatement Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Making policy or clinical recommendations that exceed the study's evidence level.
    *   **Fix**: Use hedging language ("may support," "suggests potential") unless evidence is definitive (e.g., Phase III trial).

*   **Vagueness Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Ending with "more research is needed."
    *   **Fix**: Specify *what* research is needed and *why* (e.g., "Long-term studies are needed to assess durability").

*   **Linkage Trap**:
    *   **Issue**: Discussion ends abruptly without leading into the Conclusion.
    *   **Fix**: Use the final paragraph of Discussion to set up the summary statement in the Conclusion.

## VI. Implementation Strategy for Writers

### 1. Writing Sequence
1.  **Draft Discussion First**: Complete the interpretation and limitation analysis.
2.  **Extract Core Messages**: Identify the 2-3 most important takeaways from the Discussion.
3.  **Draft Conclusion**: Synthesize these takeaways into a declarative statement.
4.  **Review Linkage**: Ensure the transition from the last paragraph of Discussion to the Conclusion is smooth.
5.  **Check Introduction**: Verify the Conclusion answers the Introduction's opening problem.

### 2. Revision Focus
*   **Cut Detail**: Remove specific mechanism details from Conclusion; keep them in Discussion.
*   **Elevate Language**: Move from specific technical terms to broader field implications.
*   **Strengthen Verbs**: Use strong, active verbs ("establishes," "defines," "clarifies") while maintaining accuracy.
*   **Ensure Closure**: Make sure the reader feels the story is complete after reading the Conclusion.

### 3. Final Verification
*   Read the Introduction, then immediately read the Conclusion. Do they match?
*   Read the Discussion end, then the Conclusion start. Is the flow logical?
*   Check for any "new" information that slipped into the Conclusion.
*   Ensure no critical limitations are completely ignored (brief acknowledgment if vital).
