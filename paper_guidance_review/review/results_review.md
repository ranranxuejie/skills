
## IV. Pitfall Avoidance Guide & Red Lines

### 1. High-Frequency Errors

*   **Error 1: Results-Discussion Mixing**.
    *   *Issue*: Interpreting *why* the result happened or comparing it to literature.
    *   *Fix*: Stick to *what* was found. Move "This suggests..." to Discussion.
    *   *Example*: "The higher accuracy indicates our model is superior..." → ✅ "Our model achieved higher accuracy..."

*   **Error 2: Text-Figure Repetition**.
    *   *Issue*: Writing out every number already present in the table/figure.
    *   *Fix*: Highlight key trends, extremes, or comparisons in text; let the table hold the dense data.
    *   *Example*: Don't list all 20 p-values in text if they are in Table 2. Summarize the range or key significant ones.

*   **Error 3: Inconsistency with Methods**.
    *   *Issue*: Reporting an analysis not described in Methods (e.g., a subgroup analysis not pre-specified).
    *   *Fix*: Ensure every result has a methodological counterpart. Label post-hoc analyses clearly.
    *   *Example*: If Methods say "ANOVA", Results shouldn't show "t-tests" without explanation.

*   **Error 4: Selective Reporting**.
    *   *Issue*: Only reporting significant findings while hiding non-significant primary outcomes.
    *   *Fix*: Report all pre-specified outcomes. Non-significant results are still results.
    *   *Example*: If Primary Outcome was X, report X even if p>0.05.

*   **Error 5: Logical Disorganization**.
    *   *Issue*: Jumping between topics without clear transitions.
    *   *Fix*: Follow the order of hypotheses or methods. Use subheadings.

### 2. Adaptation: Comprehensive vs. Specialized Journals

*   **Nature/Science/Cell**:
    *   **Style**: Narrative-driven. Results often merged with brief methodological context.
    *   **Length**: Concise. Heavy reliance on main figures (4-6).
    *   **Focus**: Broad impact findings. Detailed stats often in Methods/Supplementary.
    *   **Requirement**: Data availability statements linked in results.

*   **Specialized Journals (e.g., *PLOS ONE*, *JAMA*, *IEEE Transactions*)**:
    *   **Style**: Structured, detailed, standalone.
    *   **Length**: Can be longer. More tables allowed.
    *   **Focus**: Complete statistical reporting within the text.
    *   **Requirement**: Strict adherence to reporting checklists (CONSORT/STROBE).

### 3. Absolute Red Lines (Strictly Forbidden)

*   **Data Fabrication/Falsification**: Inventing data points, manipulating images, or cherry-picking samples.
*   **P-Hacking**: Running multiple tests until significance is found without correction.
*   **HARKing**: Hypothesizing After Results are Known (presenting post-hoc findings as pre-specified).
*   **Duplicate Publication**: Reporting the same results in multiple papers without cross-reference.
*   **Privacy Breach**: Revealing identifiable patient information in text or images.
*   **Subjective Language**: Using emotional or promotional language ("amazing", "perfect", "unfortunately").
*   **Discussion Content**: Explaining mechanisms, implications, or limitations in the Results section.

## V. Top-Tier Results Section Quality Checklist

Before submission, verify your Results section against these criteria:

### 1. Objectivity
- [ ] Is the language purely descriptive without interpretation or speculation?
- [ ] Are subjective adjectives (e.g., "interesting", "surprising") removed?
- [ ] Are causal claims avoided (unless supported by RCT design)?
- [ ] Is the tone neutral and professional?

### 2. Matchability (Methods-Results Alignment)
- [ ] Does every result correspond to a method described in the Methods section?
- [ ] Are all pre-specified primary and secondary outcomes reported?
- [ ] Are statistical tests used in Results consistent with those planned in Methods?
- [ ] Are all figures and tables referenced in the text?

### 3. Logic
- [ ] Does the order of results follow a logical progression (Primary -> Secondary -> Robustness)?
- [ ] Are subheadings clear and descriptive?
- [ ] Do transitions between paragraphs guide the reader smoothly?
- [ ] Is the main finding highlighted early in the section?

### 4. Rigor
- [ ] Are exact p-values reported (not just thresholds)?
- [ ] Are Confidence Intervals included for effect sizes?
- [ ] Are sample sizes (n) clearly stated for each experiment/analysis?
- [ ] Are error bars defined (SD vs. SEM)?
- [ ] Are missing data and exclusions accounted for?
- [ ] Are non-significant findings reported honestly?

### 5. Norms
- [ ] Are units of measurement standard and consistent?
- [ ] Are abbreviations defined at first use?
- [ ] Is the tense correct (Past for findings, Present for figures)?
- [ ] Is the word count within journal limits?
- [ ] Are supplementary results clearly distinguished from main results?
- [ ] Do figures/tables stand alone with complete legends (though detailed in Results text)?
