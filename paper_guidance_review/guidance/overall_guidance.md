# Overall Guidance

- Use concise, evidence-based academic style.
- Keep terms consistent across sections.
- For each claim, provide rationale and boundary conditions.
- Treat existing material as the source of truth for experimental facts, numeric results, dataset details, baseline names, tables, and figures.
- You may summarize, regroup, compare, or aggregate only information already present in the approved materials and previously generated sections; do not add, alter, or beautify values.
- If evidence is missing, omit the claim or keep it strictly qualitative within supported bounds; never fill gaps with invented data, extra experiments, or unverified citations.
